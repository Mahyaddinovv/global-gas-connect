1. Market Analysis

Manual communication methods such as phone calls and messaging apps (e.g., WhatsApp) are still widely used in gas delivery services. However, these methods are not scalable and often lead to poor tracking, missed requests, and inefficient coordination between customers and service providers.

Efficient request management systems are needed to improve operational reliability and service speed.

2. Competitors

1. WhatsApp / Phone Orders
Widely used in small businesses
No structured tracking of requests
No analytics or history management
High risk of human error
2. Generic CRM Systems (HubSpot, Zoho CRM)

Feature-rich and powerful
Not optimized for operational gas logistics
Require training and onboarding
Too complex for small service teams

3. Delivery Management Systems
Designed for fleet and logistics companies
Focused on route optimization and delivery tracking
Not adapted for inquiry-based gas cylinder workflows

3. User Interviews (Simulated but Realistic)

The following stakeholder groups were considered:

- Restaurant owner  
- Workshop manager  
- Delivery coordinator  
- Customer service representative  
Key findings:
- Users need fast and simple request tracking  
- A centralized dashboard is essential for coordination  
- Simplicity is more important than feature complexity  
- Existing tools are either too manual or too complex  
4. Differentiator

Global Gas Connect is a domain-specific operational CRM designed specifically for gas service workflows.

Unlike generic CRM systems or manual communication tools, it is:

- lightweight and easy to use  
- focused only on gas service operations  
- optimized for inquiry-based request handling  
- designed for speed, clarity, and operational efficiency
